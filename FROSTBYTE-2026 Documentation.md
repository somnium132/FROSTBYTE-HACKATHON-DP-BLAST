**FROSTBYTE**  
Break the Ice. Build the Future.

Implementing Office: City Youth Development Office (CYDO), City Government of Santa Rosa, Laguna

1. # **BACKGROUND AND RATIONALE**

   **FROSTBYTE: Youth Week Hackathon** is the City of Santa Rosa's flagship youth innovation competition, organized under the annual Linggo ng Kabataan celebration, that brings together passionate and innovative young minds to develop creative, technology–based solutions to real–world community challenges. Designed as a platform for learning, collaboration, and innovation, the hackathon empowers the youth to showcase their skills in coding, design, and critical thinking while contributing to meaningful change in their communities.

   Unlike a typical ideathon, **teams are expected to move beyond concept and pitch decks**: through registration, team building, and **three progressive competition rounds** in July 2026, teams of five to seven members, composed of in–school youth, out–of–school youth, and young professionals aged 15 to 30, will build a working prototype that they will demo live at the Final Round on 10th of the August 2026\.

   Participating projects are encouraged to be grounded in the **actual context, needs, problems, and development priorities** of the **City of Santa Rosa, Laguna**, so that proposed solutions remain relevant, localized, and useful to the community. 

   The activity is anchored in the mandate of the **City Youth Development Office (CYDO)** to develop, empower, and promote the active participation of the youth sector and is likewise aligned with the United Nations Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and the Ten Centers of Youth Participation. Each participating team is required to align its project with at least one SDG and to ensure that all ideation, coding, and design work is original and developed within the official hackathon period.

   Ultimately, FROSTBYTE serves as an investment in youth development by cultivating a generation of innovators who are prepared to apply technology and creativity in service of their communities, while gaining practical experience in teamwork, problem–solving, and public presentation.

2. # **OBJECTIVES**

   ## **General Objective:**

   To provide the youth of Santa Rosa City with a platform to apply technology, creativity, and collaborative problem–solving to develop practical solutions to real community challenges.

   ## **Specific Objectives:**

   By the end of FROSTBYTE: Youth Week Hackathon 2026, participants will be able to:

1. Apply coding, design, and critical thinking skills to develop technology–based solutions to real–world community challenges.  
2. Align project concepts with at least one UN Sustainable Development Goal and a Center of Youth Participation.  
3. Strengthen teamwork, collaboration, and communication skills through the ideation, prototyping, and pitching process.  
4. Gain hands–on experience in presenting and defending a project before a panel of judges and an audience.  
5. Cultivate a spirit of innovation, civic engagement, and service to the community through technology.

3. # **PROJECT DESCRIPTION AND MECHANICS**

* ## **Competition guidelines:**

  * Projects must be technology-based solutions addressing a real community challenge, preferably rooted in the local context, needs, or problems of Santa Rosa City, Laguna.   
  * Projects must align with at least one UN Sustainable Development Goal and one Center of Youth Participation.  
  * Ideation, coding, design, and major project development must be original and completed within the official hackathon period.  
  * Teams must submit a project brief and prototype materials according to the official submission guide.  
  * Code–based teams are encouraged to maintain a public repository with a clear README and version history.  
  * Non–code teams may submit visually complete prototypes, wireframes, UI mockups, hardware models, or other acceptable proof–of–concept materials.

* ## **Acceptable Project Formats**

  * Website or web application  
  * Website or web extension  
  * Game or interactive digital experience  
  * Internet of Things (IoT) solution  
  * Robotics or hardware prototype  
  * Data or AI–assisted solution  
  * Other tech–based innovations approved by the organizers

* ## **Rulebook**

  * Teams must submit only original work created during the official hackathon period.  
  * Pre–existing complete projects are **NOT** allowed.  
  * Teams may use open–source tools, templates, assets, and AI tools, provided that their use is documented and does not violate licenses or competition rules.  
  * Plagiarized, offensive, discriminatory, unsafe, or harmful content is strictly prohibited.  
  * Projects must not promote partisan political campaigns, hate speech, or unauthorized commercial advertising.  
  * All participants must observe respect, professionalism, and fairness throughout the event.  
  * The organizers may document the event and use photos, videos, and approved participant materials for official non–commercial promotions.  
  * Judges' decisions are final.

* ## **Team Formation and Registration Requirements:** 

  * Open to youth aged 15 to 30\.  
  * Each team must have five (5) members.  
  * Open to in–school youth, out–of–school youth, and young professionals.  
  * All team members must comply with the official registration requirements.  
  * Participants under 18 years old must submit a signed parental consent form if required by the final registration guidelines.  
  * Each participant may join only one team.

8. # **MONITORING AND EVALUATION PLAN**

* ## **Attendance and registration tracking method:**

- Participant registration shall be monitored through the official registration form and verified by the Operations Committee. Each team shall submit complete participant information, including team name, member details, age, school or organization affiliation, contact information, and required supporting documents.

- During every major phase of the hackathon, attendance shall be tracked through official attendance sheets or digital check–in forms. These may include separate attendance records for orientation, elimination activities, semifinal activities, and the final pitching, judging, and awarding ceremony. The Operations Committee shall consolidate the attendance records and submit the final participant attendance report to the organizing committee.


* ## **Judging and scoring the documentation process:** 

- The Executive Committee shall prepare official scoring sheets based on the approved judging criteria for the elimination round, semifinal round, and final round. Each judge shall independently evaluate the teams using the official rubric.

- Scores shall be recorded, checked, and consolidated by the assigned tabulation team. The Executive Committee shall review the final scores to ensure accuracy, fairness, and completeness. All judging sheets, score summaries, and final rankings shall be documented and kept as part of the official event records.

* ## **Post–activity evaluation form/participant feedback survey:** 

- After the activity, participants, judges, resource speakers, volunteers, partners, and selected guests may be asked to accomplish a post–activity evaluation form. The feedback form shall gather responses on the overall event experience, clarity of mechanics, usefulness of the activity, quality of facilitation, venue and logistics, judging process, and areas for improvement.  
- The results of the evaluation shall be summarized by the organizing committee and used as a basis for improving future editions of FROSTBYTE and other youth innovation programs.  
- 

* ## **Success indicators (e.g., number of working prototypes completed, SDG alignment rate, participant satisfaction rating):** 

  The success of **FROSTBYTE: Youth Week Hackathon 2026** shall be measured using the following indicators:

- Number of registered teams and participants  
- Number of teams that completed the elimination round.  
- Number of teams that advanced to the semifinal and final rounds.  
- Number of working prototypes, proof–of–concept outputs, or project demonstrations completed  
- Percentage of projects aligned with at least one United Nations Sustainable Development Goal  
- Percentage of projects aligned with at least one Center of Youth   
- Completion and documentation of official judging sheets and score summaries  
- Participant attendance rate during required activities  
- Participant satisfaction rating based on the post–activity evaluation form  
- Number of partner organizations, schools, sponsors, speakers, and judges engaged.  
- Quality and feasibility of submitted projects based on judges’ feedback.  
- Documentation of event photos, videos, reports, and other required outputs.

9. # **SUSTAINABILITY PLAN**

* ## **Plans for continuing or scaling FROSTBYTE in future years:** 

- The City Youth Development Office, with the support of the City Government of Santa Rosa and partner organizations, may continue FROSTBYTE as an annual youth innovation program under the Linggo ng Kabataan celebration. Future editions may expand the number of participating teams, introduce more specific problem tracks, invite more schools and youth organizations, and include additional mentoring sessions before the final competition.

- The organizing committee may also develop FROSTBYTE into a wider innovation pipeline by adding preparatory workshops, online mentoring sessions, project consultation days, and post–event showcases. This will help participants improve their technical, design, research, and presentation skills before and after the competition.


* ## **Potential partnerships/sponsorships (e.g., private sector, academe, AWS Learning Club – Polar, other tech organizations):** 

- To strengthen the sustainability of the program, FROSTBYTE may continue building partnerships with schools, colleges, universities, youth organizations, technology communities, private companies, local businesses, media partners, and civic organizations.

- Potential partners may include the academe, private sector sponsors, AWS Learning Club – Polar, technology communities, student organizations, professional volunteers, and other institutions that support youth development, digital innovation, entrepreneurship, and community service.

- These partnerships may provide resource speakers, mentors, judges, prizes, training support, promotional assistance, learning resources, technical guidance, and post–hackathon opportunities for participants.


* ## **Post–hackathon support for winning teams/prototypes:** 

- **Winning teams are strictly required to actively participate in the post–hackathon development phase** to refine, test, and scale their MVPs (Minimum Viable Products) into production–ready systems.

- To support this phase, the development, optimization, and scaling of these projects will be fully funded by the Santa Rosa Local Government Unit (LGU), subject to approved budget allocations and milestone reviews.

- The finalized systems will be directly integrated into the target Santa Rosa government departments or localized public–sector frameworks to solve the specific community challenges identified in the hackathon tracks.

- In consideration of the financial funding, institutional resources, and support provided by the city, all winning systems and software architectures will be officially **patented and owned by the City Government of Santa Rosa, Local Government Unit (LGU)**. Original team members will be credited as authors/inventors, but exclusive operational and intellectual property rights will reside with the LGU.

- To prevent project abandonment or stalled maintenance due to unavailable student developers (e.g., academic transitions, graduation, or external constraints), the development lifecycle will be strictly monitored.

- The City LGU, in coordination with technical supervisors and management committees, reserves the right to transition codebases to designated LGU IT personnel or secondary technical teams to guarantee continuous system deployment, maintenance, and long–term uptime.
